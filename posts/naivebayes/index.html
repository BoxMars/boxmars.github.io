Conditional Probability and Bayesian rule

Since $$P(A|B)=\frac{P(A\cap B)}{P(B)}$$ We can get $$P(A \cap B)=P(A|B)P(B)$$ and $$P(A \cap B)=P(B|A)P(A)$$ If we have $n$ events $B_i$ which is indenpendent, i.e. $$\forall B_i, \forall B_j, B_i\cap B_j=\emptyset$$ and $$\cup_{i=1}^n B_i=S$$ Then $$P(A \cap B_1)=P(A|B_1)P(B_1)$$ $$P(A \cap B_2)=P(A|B_2)P(B_2)$$ $$P(A \cap B_3)=P(A|B_3)P(B_3)$$ $$…$$ $$P(A \cap B_n)=P(A|B_n)P(B_n)$$ $$\sum_{i=1}^n P(A\cap B_i)=\sum_{i=1}^nP(A|B_i)P(B_i)$$ We get the formula $$P(A)=\sum_{i=1}^nP(A|B_i)P(B_i)$$

Bayesian reasoning

$$P(H|E)=\frac{P(E|H)P(H)}{P(E|H)P(H)+P(E|\neg H)P(\neg H)}$$ where

  • $P(H)$ is the prior probability oh the hypothesis $H$ being true
  • $P(E|H)$ is the probability that hypothesis $H$ being yrue will result in evidence $E$
  • $P(\neg H)$ is the prior probability of hypothesis $H$ being false
  • $P(E|\neg H)$ is the probability of finding evidence $E$ even when hypothesis $H$ is false
