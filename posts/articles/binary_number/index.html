Binary Number

Binary Terms

  • A binary digit is called a bit (BInary digiT)
  • A group of four bits is called a nibble
  • A group of eight bits (or two nibbles) is called a byte
  • The Most significant bit (MSB) is the left-most bit
  • The least significant bit (LSB) is the right-most bit

Representing Signed Binary Numbers

Binary numbers which can only represent 0 or positive numbers are known as unsigned numbers

Binary numbers which can represent positive, 0, and negative numbers are known as signed numbers

Sign and Magnitude

MSB is used as a sign bit ( $0$ for positive and $1$ for negative)

For example (for 8-bit sign and magnitude signed numbers):

  • The negative of $00010010$ is $10010010$ (in decimal is $-18$)
  • The negative of $11001111$ is $01001111$ (in decimal is $+79$)
  • The decimal value of $10000011$ is $-3$ (hint: ignore the sign-bit and do the conversion first)

One’s Complement

The negative of a given binary number is formed by taking the complement of each bit in the number.

MSB still indicates the sign of the number

  • The negative of $00010001$ is $11101110$) (decimal value $-17$)
  • The negative of $11111110$ is $00000001$) (decimal value $1$)
  • The decimal value of $11110101$ is $-10$ (hint: find the corresponding positive value and do the conversion)

Two’s Complement

The negative of a given binary number is formed by taking the complement of each bit in the number, then add 1 to the result.

MSB still indicates the sign of the number
